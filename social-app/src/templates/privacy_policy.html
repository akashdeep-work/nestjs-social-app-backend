1) Who we are

“Social App” ("we", "us", or "our") is operated by Happiibook LLC, located at Happiibook LLC dba Social App, 1810 E. Sahara Ave Suite 212, Las Vegas, Nevada 89104. Our data protection contact is Will Pryor (w.pryor@happiibook.com). Where applicable, our Data Protection Officer (DPO) can be reached at w.pryor@happiibook.com.

This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal data when you use our professional networking features similar to LinkedIn (e.g., profiles, posts, messages, connections, jobs, and events).

2) Scope

This policy applies to our websites, mobile apps, APIs, and related services (collectively, the “Services”). By using the Services, you acknowledge that we will process your data as described here. If you do not agree, please discontinue use.

3) Data we collect

A. You provide

  • Account data: name, email address, password (hashed), profile photo, headline, summary, education, skills, location, and preferences.
  • Content: posts, comments, messages, endorsements, reactions, media uploads.
  • Professional information: work history, certifications, portfolio, resume/CV, job preferences.
  • Payments: billing details and transaction identifiers (processed by our payment provider; we do not store full card numbers).

B. Automatically collected

  • Device and usage data: IP address, device identifiers, app version, browser type, pages viewed, referring/exit pages, timestamps, interactions.
  • Approximate location inferred from IP address or device settings.
  • Cookies, pixels, SDKs (see Cookies & tracking).

C. From others

  • Contacts and connections (if you choose to import or connect address books).
  • Third-party services you connect, including social logins (see Social logins).
  • Partners, analytics, advertisers, and public sources (e.g., public profiles, company pages).
Sensitive data: We do not require sensitive data (e.g., health, biometrics). Do not include such information in your profile or content unless you choose to make it public and understand the risks.

4) Sources of data

We collect data directly from you, automatically via the Services, and from third parties you authorize or where the data is publicly available.

5) How we use data

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Services, including personalization of feeds and recommendations.
  • Facilitate social features: connections, messaging, endorsements, events, and job applications.
  • Account authentication, fraud prevention, and safety, including detecting suspicious login activity.
  • Communications: service announcements, updates, marketing (with opt-out), and customer support.
  • Analytics, research, and product development.
  • Compliance with legal obligations and enforcement of terms, including investigating abuse.

6) Legal bases (EEA/UK)

Where EU/UK laws apply, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Contract: to provide the Services you request.
  • Legitimate interests: to secure, improve, and market the Services, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent: where required (e.g., certain cookies or marketing). You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligation: to comply with applicable laws.

7) Sharing & disclosures

We may share data with:

  • Service providers processing data on our behalf (hosting, analytics, customer support, payment processing).
  • Other users in accordance with your profile visibility and settings (e.g., your name, headline, posts).
  • Partners for integrated features (e.g., job boards, applicant tracking systems) with your direction or consent.
  • Legal and safety authorities if required by law or to protect rights, safety, and security.
  • Business transfers in connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale. We'll continue to protect your data per this policy.

8) Social logins (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn)

You can create or sign in to your account using Google, Facebook, or LinkedIn. When you do, the provider will authenticate you and may share with us certain information such as your name, email address, profile photo, and (where permitted) your public profile and connections. The specific data we receive depends on the provider and your settings with them.

  • What we receive: typically name, email, profile picture, and a unique provider ID. For LinkedIn, we may also receive your headline and public profile URL if you grant permission.
  • What we do not receive: your social network passwords.
  • How we use it: to create your Social App account, prefill profile details, and prevent fraud.
  • Revoking access: you can disconnect social logins in Settings → Connected accounts and via your provider's own settings.

Use of your information by these providers is governed by their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review them.

9) Cookies & tracking

We use cookies, SDKs, and similar technologies to operate the Services, remember preferences, analyze usage, and measure campaigns. Where required, we ask for your consent. You can manage preferences in our Cookie Settings and through your browser or device controls.

Categories

  • Strictly necessary (authentication, security, load balancing)
  • Functional (preferences, localization)
  • Analytics (traffic and performance measurement)
  • Advertising (ad personalization and measurement)

10) Retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described or as required by law. Typical retention periods:

  • Account data: for the life of your account and for up to 1 year after deletion for backup, audit, and legal purposes.
  • Messages and posts: until you delete them or your account is deleted, subject to technical backups.
  • Log data: typically 1 year.

11) Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal data (e.g., encryption in transit, access controls, vulnerability management). However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

Your rights depend on your location. Subject to legal limits, you may have the right to:

  • Object to or restrict processing
  • Data portability
  • Withdraw consent
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

To exercise rights, contact us at happiibook@gmail.com. We may need to verify your identity.

If you are in India, you have the right to access, correct, and delete your personal data, and to grievance redressal. We process personal data in accordance with lawful purposes under the DPDP Act and obtain consent where required.

For California residents: we collect identifiers, commercial information, internet activity, geolocation (approximate), professional information, and inferences. We disclose these categories to service providers for business purposes. We do not sell personal information in the traditional sense. We may “share” personal information for cross‑context behavioral advertising.

Your rights include access, deletion, correction, and limiting use of sensitive personal information. To exercise, email at happiibook@gmail.com. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

15) Children

Our Services are not directed to children under 13 years. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided data, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

16) International data transfers

We may process and store data in countries outside your own. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

17) Automated decision‑making

We may use automated systems to help rank feeds, detect spam or abuse, and recommend connections or jobs. These systems are regularly evaluated for fairness and effectiveness. You can adjust personalization settings in Settings → Privacy.

18) Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a “Do Not Track” (DNT) signal. As no uniform standard exists, we do not respond to DNT at this time. We honor applicable legal requirements for consent and opt‑outs.
